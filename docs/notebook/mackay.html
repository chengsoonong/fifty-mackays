What is one Mackay?

One Mackay is a human sized unit of power

Sir David Mackay proposed that 40W=1kWh/d is a nice human sized unit in his groundbreaking book “Without Hot Air”. We propose to name a new unit in Sir David’s honour, called the Mackay, 1 Mackay = 1 kWh/d. The 2000W society argues that 2kW is an equitable amount of power for society. This implies that we should be targetting about 50 Mackays person. However, for personal planning purposes, 60 Mackays (=2.3kW) is a more practical number because 60 can be divided in many ways.

Therefore 60 Mackays is a useful way to think about household power use.

40 [W] Watts is the same as 0.96 [W.s] kWh/d.
Therefore we define 1 Mackay = 1 kWh/d = 38.4 [W] Watts.
